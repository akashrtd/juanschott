## The State of Earth

Earth is no longer the planet humanity once knew.

Centuries of failed Vimana cycles have left cumulative scars across every system — atmospheric, geological, orbital, biological. Each cycle that concludes without a victor does not merely reset the arena. It extracts a price from the world below. Energy discharges have stripped atmosphere from vast regions. Gravitational shearing has fractured continental shelves. Thermal venting from the Vimana itself has boiled seas and flash-frozen others in the same cycle. The planet bears these wounds the way old skin bears burns — uneven, permanent, and deeply wrong.

### Atmospheric and Surface Damage

Roughly sixty percent of Earth's surface is now classified as **dead zone** — regions where the atmosphere has been thinned beyond the threshold for unaided human survival. In the worst corridors, stretching across what was once northern Asia and the interior of North America, the air is so thin that pressure suits are required even at ground level. These regions are not merely empty; they are hostile. Static discharge from residual Vimana energy fields arcs unpredictably across flat terrain. Dust storms carry irradiated particulate matter at speeds that can strip flesh from bone. Nothing grows there. Nothing lives there by choice.

The southern portions of Africa, South America, and the Indian subcontinent have been reduced to **irradiated wasteland**. Whether through deliberate punishment by the Pantheon or incidental fallout from catastrophic cycle failures, these regions carry radiation levels that preclude permanent habitation. Scavenger crews venture in for salvage — recovered technology, mineral deposits, sometimes the remains of pre-cycle cities — but they do not stay. The radiation is unpredictable. Pockets of intensity shift with seasonal weather patterns, and what reads as safe one month can become lethal the next.

Large portions of the Atlantic and Pacific have become **frozen oceans**, their thermal balance disrupted by orbital shifts and energy bleed from the Vimana. These frozen expanses are navigable on foot in some seasons, though doing so is enormously dangerous. Icequakes, pressure ridges, and buried thermal vents make the surface unstable. Beneath the ice, deep ocean currents still move — sluggish, cold, but alive. Some fishing communities have established ice-drilling operations along the margins where frozen ocean meets open water, harvesting what deep-sea life persists.

Conversely, portions of the Mediterranean and the South China Sea have **boiled away** entirely, leaving behind vast salt flats and mineral deserts. These regions are hot, dry, and chemically hostile. The salt flats are mined for industrial use by arcology supply chains, but the work is grueling and dangerous.

### Orbital Instability

Earth's orbit has been shifted multiple times over the course of the cycles. This is not metaphor. The Vimana's arrivals and departures — its gravitational influence during orbital insertion and extraction — have nudged the planet's trajectory in ways that accumulate. The current orbit is approximately 1.3 million kilometers closer to the sun than pre-cycle astronomical records indicate. The eccentricity of the orbit has increased as well, producing seasons that do not align with any historical calendar.

The length of a day has changed. It is now approximately 25 hours and 12 minutes, an increase attributed to rotational deceleration caused by repeated gravitational shearing. The sun appears approximately four percent larger in the sky than it did in pre-cycle records. The combined effect is subtle enough that a single human lifetime does not notice the drift, but the Scholar Caste has maintained precise measurements across generations. The numbers do not lie. Earth is not where it was. It is not turning as it was. The sky itself is evidence of accumulated cosmic violence.

### Gravity Anomalies

When the Vimana is in orbit — during active cycle periods — localized gravity anomalies persist across the planet's surface. These manifest as regions where apparent gravity is measurably stronger or weaker than baseline. In the strongest anomalies, objects fall at accelerated rates, structural materials fatigue faster, and human physiology is stressed beyond comfortable limits. In the weakest zones, loose objects drift, water does not pour correctly, and vertigo is common.

The anomalies are not random. They correlate with the Vimana's orbital position and appear to be caused by gravitational lensing effects from the structure itself. The Scholar Caste has mapped them extensively and updates anomaly forecasts in real time during active cycles. Arcology cities are built with gravitational compensators precisely because of this phenomenon. Rural communities have no such protection.

## The Habitable Zones

Humanity persists in pockets. The habitable zones are defined not by what is comfortable, but by what is survivable.

### Shielded Arcology Cities

The arcologies are humanity's greatest architectural achievement and its most visible dependency on Pantheon-rewarded technology. There are seventeen major arcologies currently operational, each housing between 500,000 and 4 million inhabitants. They are massive enclosed structures — some rising over two kilometers in height, others extending an equivalent distance underground. Their outer shells are constructed from **torsion-cast alloys**, a material science reward earned during Cycle 3,187, which provides exceptional strength-to-weight ratios and inherent radiation shielding.

Inside an arcology, the environment is controlled. Atmosphere is scrubbed and recycled. Temperature is regulated. Artificial lighting simulates diurnal cycles. Agriculture occurs in vertical hydroponic bays. Water is reclaimed from every possible source. The arcologies are not luxurious by pre-cycle standards, but they are stable, and in the current state of the world, stability is the highest currency.

Each arcology is governed independently. There is no arcology union, no central authority. Alliances form and dissolve based on resource needs, trade agreements, and mutual defense pacts. Some arcologies are open and democratic; others are authoritarian, ruled by guild leaderships or hereditary councils. The only universal feature is the presence of a **Fighter Guild hall** — a designated facility for training, evaluating, and selecting candidates for the Vimana.

### The Equatorial Belt

The equatorial belt — roughly defined as the region between 15 degrees north and 15 degrees south latitude — remains the warmest and most naturally habitable zone on the planet. The atmosphere here is thickest, radiation levels are at their lowest, and enough natural rainfall persists to support agriculture without extensive technological intervention. This is where the majority of Earth's remaining natural ecosystem survives: scrub forests, grasslands, hardy wetlands, and the last vestiges of coral reef in shallow equatorial waters.

Communities in the equatorial belt range from small farming villages to moderate-sized towns of up to 100,000 people. They are typically less technologically advanced than the arcologies, though not primitive. Solar power, basic communications, and firearm-level weaponry are common. Medical care is rudimentary compared to arcology standards — broken bones are set, infections are treated with available antibiotics, but advanced regenerative medicine is unavailable outside trade agreements with arcology-aligned guilds.

### Coastal Remnants

Where oceans have not frozen or boiled away, coastal communities persist. These are fishing settlements, salvage ports, and trade hubs, typically numbering no more than 50,000 inhabitants. They are defined by their relationship to the sea — a relationship that is now deeply uncertain. Ocean currents have shifted. Fish populations have migrated or collapsed. What was a reliable fishery one generation may be empty the next.

Coastal communities are the primary interface between arcology supply chains and the broader human population. They serve as ports for cargo haulers moving goods between arcologies and as collection points for raw materials harvested from the dead zones and wastelands. They are culturally distinct — tough, pragmatic, and deeply suspicious of both arcology authority and the Vimana selection process.

### Underground Cities

Built deep beneath the surface to escape radiation, the underground cities are marvels of desperation engineering. They range from converted pre-cycle mining installations to purpose-excavated cavern networks housing hundreds of thousands. Power comes primarily from geothermal sources — the one energy supply that the Vimana cycles have not disrupted, because it comes from within the planet itself.

Underground cities are dim. Artificial lighting is rationed. The air smells of mineral dust and recycled atmosphere. Space is at a premium, and the social structures that emerge in these environments tend toward collectivism out of necessity — shared resources, shared space, shared labor. They are the most culturally isolated of the habitable zones, with limited trade connections to the surface and a distinct dialectic tradition that sometimes borders on its own language.

### The Technology Divide

The single most significant factor separating human communities is access to technology. Arcology cities possess Pantheon-rewarded technology: energy weapons, advanced medical systems, gravitational compensators, and materials that pre-cycle science could not produce. Rural equatorial communities may have basic electricity and mechanical tools. Underground cities sit somewhere in between, with strong geothermal engineering but limited access to Pantheon-derived systems.

This divide is not merely economic. It is existential. When a child is born with a treatable genetic condition in an arcology, they receive regenerative therapy and live a full life. When the same child is born in a coastal village, they may not survive infancy. The arcology populations know this. The rural populations know this. The resentment is real, and it shapes politics, culture, and the selection of Vimana fighters in ways that ripple across every cycle.

## How Humanity Organizes

There is no unified Earth government. There has not been one since the early cycles dissolved the last remnants of the United Nations into irrelevance. Humanity is fragmented into **city-states** and **regional alliances**, each with its own governance structure, territorial claims, and strategic interests.

### The Fighter Guilds

The Fighter Guilds are the most powerful organizations on Earth that are not governmental bodies. They are dedicated to the training, evaluation, and selection of Vimana competitors. Their influence extends far beyond combat preparation — guilds control significant economic resources, maintain intelligence networks across arcologies and rural zones alike, and serve as the primary diplomatic intermediaries between communities during cycle preparations.

Different guilds maintain fundamentally different philosophies regarding the nature of the Vimana and the optimal approach to competition:

- **Purist Guilds** believe that the Vimana tests the essential human spirit. They reject bio-enhancement and cybernetic modification for their fighters, arguing that the Pantheon rewards authenticity and that artificial advantage invites artificial punishment. Their fighters are trained through extreme physical conditioning, martial discipline, and mental fortitude techniques.

- **Augmentation Guilds** take the opposite position. They argue that the Vimana is a test of adaptation — that humanity must evolve to meet the challenge, not merely endure it. Their fighters undergo extensive bio-enhancement: accelerated reflex pathways, reinforced skeletal structures, optimized metabolic systems. Some augmentation guilds push further, exploring cybernetic integration and neural expansion.

- **Synthesis Guilds** occupy a middle ground, combining natural human capability with selective, targeted enhancement. They are the most common guild type and typically produce the most consistent — if not the most spectacular — fighter performance.

Guild rivalries are intense and sometimes violent. Territory disputes over recruitment grounds, philosophical disagreements over fighter preparation, and competition for Pantheon-rewarded resources all fuel ongoing tensions. During cycle preparation periods, these rivalries can escalate into open conflict — though most arcology governments enforce strict non-violence treaties during active selection windows.

### The Scholar Caste

The Scholar Caste is a trans-national community of scientists and researchers dedicated to studying Pantheon-rewarded technology. Their primary mission is **reverse-engineering** — attempting to understand the fundamental principles behind the energy weapons, materials, medical systems, and other technologies that have been granted as cycle rewards.

Their work is slow. Pantheon technology does not yield easily to human analysis. Materials that appear to be simple alloys exhibit behaviors that defy current models of physics. Energy systems operate on principles that the Scholars can observe but not replicate. Three thousand years of accumulated rewards, and the Scholars have achieved perhaps a twelve-percent understanding of the underlying science. That twelve percent has been enough to build the arcologies, develop advanced medical treatments, and create the weapons that arcology militias carry. The remaining eighty-eight percent is a locked door behind which humanity's future might — or might not — exist.

The Scholar Caste maintains archives in every major arcology and operates independent research facilities in shielded locations. They are politically neutral by charter, though in practice their reliance on arcology funding creates dependencies that shape their research priorities.

### The Witness Corps

The Witness Corps is perhaps humanity's most important institution and certainly its most somber. Their mandate is simple: **document everything**. Every cycle. Every fighter. Every match. Every outcome. Every reward. Every punishment.

The Witnesses maintain the **Generational Record** — a continuously compiled archive of Vimana cycle data stretching back to the earliest documented cycles. This record includes fighter profiles, combat analysis, arena descriptions, Pantheon communications, reward distributions, ecological impact assessments, and the personal testimonies of survivors. It is humanity's collective memory of its own subjugation, preserved with the meticulous care of people who understand that forgetting is the first step toward extinction.

Witness historians are trained from youth in memory techniques, data preservation, and objective documentation methodology. They are embedded in every arcology, every guild, every fighter selection process. They do not interfere. They record. Their neutrality is sacred — even guilds that agree on nothing else agree that the Witnesses must be protected and their records kept inviolate.

### Cultural Attitudes Toward the Vimana

Humanity's relationship with the Vimana is not monolithic. Attitudes vary wildly across regions, cultures, and individuals:

- **Sacred Test** — Some communities view the Vimana as a divine trial, a crucible through which humanity must pass to prove its worth. These cultures tend to produce the most willing volunteers and the most elaborate fighter preparation rituals. Selection is an honor. Participation is a blessing. Death in the Vimana is martyrdom.

- **Cosmic Oppression** — Others view the Vimana as pure exploitation — an alien power using humanity as entertainment or experiment. These cultures resist the selection process, harbor fighters who flee conscription, and channel resources into anti-Pantheon research. Their fighters are often sent under protest, and some have entered the Vimana with explicitly defiant objectives.

- **Opportunity** — A pragmatic third view treats the Vimana as a transaction. Fighters go in. If they succeed, rewards come out. Those rewards build civilizations. The calculus is brutal but honest: the suffering of the few purchases the survival of the many. Communities that hold this view tend to invest heavily in fighter preparation and guild support, viewing the cycle as a competitive marketplace.

Most individuals hold some combination of these views. The tension between them defines much of Earth's internal politics.

## The Fighter Selection Process

Each Vimana cycle, the **Invitation** is transmitted — a Pantheon communication that specifies the number of fighters required, any special conditions or restrictions for the coming cycle, and the timeline for delivery. The Invitation is received simultaneously by every arcology, every guild, every Witness station. It cannot be ignored. It cannot be negotiated. The number it specifies must be met.

### Methods of Selection

How communities fill their fighter quota varies enormously:

**Volunteer systems** are the most common in equatorial and coastal communities. Fighters step forward willingly, motivated by a combination of cultural conditioning, personal conviction, and — in some cases — the material rewards promised to their families if they perform well. The volunteer pool is rarely sufficient to meet the full quota.

**Tournaments** are used by guilds and arcology governments to identify the strongest candidates. These range from formal combat competitions to multi-discipline trials testing physical capability, tactical thinking, and psychological resilience. Tournament selection is generally considered the most meritocratic approach, though it inevitably favors those with access to better training and nutrition.

**Conscription** is used by communities that cannot fill their quota through volunteers or tournaments. The criteria for conscription vary — some select the physically strongest, others select those deemed most expendable by social metrics. Conscription is deeply unpopular and is a primary driver of political unrest during selection periods.

**Purpose-built artificial humans** represent the most extreme approach. In some arcologies, bio-engineering facilities produce fighters specifically designed for a given cycle's conditions. These artificial humans are optimized for combat performance — enhanced physiology, accelerated neural processing, suppressed fear responses. They are controversial even among augmentation-friendly guilds, raising questions about personhood, consent, and whether an engineered being can truly represent humanity in a test of human worth.

### Cybersymbiotes

**Cybersymbiotes** are the rarest and most valued class of fighter. These are individuals who have undergone **cybernetic symbiosis** — a process in which a bio-compatible technological organism is integrated directly into the host's nervous system. The symbiote enhances processing speed, reaction time, sensory perception, and — in some cases — provides direct interface with certain categories of Pantheon technology.

The symbiosis process is dangerous. Compatibility rates are estimated at less than three percent of candidates. Those who fail either die or suffer catastrophic neurological damage. Those who succeed become something more — and something less — than human. Their combat performance is exceptional, consistently ranking in the upper percentile of cycle participants. But cybersymbiotes are psychologically complex: the merged identity that results from symbiosis changes how a person thinks, feels, and relates to the world. Whether this makes them better or worse suited for the Vimana is a matter of ongoing debate.

### The Weight of Selection

Being selected for the Vimana carries a dual burden. It is simultaneously the highest honor a community can bestow — the recognition that an individual has been judged worthy to represent humanity — and an effective death sentence. Historical return rates from Vimana cycles are approximately fourteen percent. Of those who return, many carry physical and psychological scars that never fully heal.

Fighters who return successfully are called **Survivors**. They become cultural icons, political figures, and — often — deeply troubled individuals. The weight of what they experienced in the Vimana, compounded by the knowledge that their survival was purchased by the deaths of others, creates a psychological profile that the Witness Corps has documented extensively but never fully resolved.

## The Cultural Impact of the Cycles

The Vimana cycles are not merely an event in human history. They are the defining context of human civilization. Every aspect of culture — art, religion, family structure, education, language — is shaped by the knowledge that the cycles continue, that the next Invitation will come, that someone's child will be called.

### Art and Music

The dominant themes of human creative expression are **cosmic insignificance, defiance, and sacrifice**. Visual art favors stark contrasts — the tiny human figure against the vast alien structure, the single fighter standing in an impossible arena. Music is divided between dirges and battle anthems, with little middle ground. The most celebrated works are those that capture the tension between despair and resistance, the refusal to accept that humanity's fate is fixed.

Underground cities have developed unique musical traditions built around resonant frequencies in their cavern environments — deep, percussive, rhythmic music that physically vibrates through the rock. Equatorial communities produce oral poetry traditions that stretch for hours, reciting the histories of cycles past. Arcology elites collect pre-cycle art as status symbols, preserved fragments of a world that no longer exists.

### Religious Fracture

Pre-cycle religions persist in modified forms, but the Vimana has shattered every theology. The central question — **why does this happen to us?** — admits no easy answer, and the divisions it creates are profound:

- **Pantheon Worshippers** believe the Vimana's creators are divine beings whose purposes are beyond human comprehension. Worship takes the form of ritual submission, elaborate ceremonies of gratitude for the rewards that are granted, and theological frameworks that position human suffering as a necessary component of cosmic order.

- **Rejectionists** deny any divinity in the Pantheon. They view the Vimana's creators as powerful but mortal aliens — oppressors to be resisted, not gods to be worshipped. Rejectionist theology centers on human dignity and the moral imperative to refuse cooperation, even when refusal carries terrible costs.

- **Testamentalists** occupy a middle position, viewing the cycles as a genuine test with a genuine end. They believe that successful completion — a sufficient number of cycle victories — will result in humanity's liberation. Their faith is forward-looking, focused on the possibility that the next cycle might be the last.

### The Concept of "JuanSchott"

Across all cultures, all languages, all divisions, one concept has become universal: **JuanSchott**.

The term translates roughly to **"One Shot."** It refers to the fighter who enters the Vimana and returns — the one who takes their single opportunity and converts it into victory. JuanSchott is not merely a word. It is an archetype, a cultural ideal, a mythic standard against which every fighter is measured.

To be called JuanSchott is the highest informal recognition a fighter can receive. It means perfection. It means the one shot landed, the one decision made correctly, the one moment of human brilliance that defeated an impossible challenge. Every guild trains toward it. Every fighter dreams of it. Every culture tells stories about the ones who achieved it.

The concept has expanded beyond combat. In common usage, "taking the JuanSchott" means committing fully to a single decisive action — the all-in, the no-return, the moment where everything is risked on one attempt. It is used in business, in politics, in personal relationships. It is the closest thing humanity has to a universal cultural value: the belief that a single moment of perfect execution can outweigh a lifetime of failure.

### Growing Up Under the Cycles

Children born into the current era are shaped by the Vimana from birth. In guild families, combat training begins as early as age five — not because children are expected to fight, but because the physical and mental conditioning required takes a lifetime to develop. In rural communities, children learn to identify radiation zones, read atmospheric quality indicators, and navigate dead zone terrain before they learn advanced mathematics. In arcology populations, children are educated in Pantheon technology, cycle history, and the strategic analysis of past arena matches.

Every child knows that they might be called. The Invitation does not discriminate by age beyond a minimum threshold. The possibility sits in the background of every life decision — what to study, where to live, who to love — like a shadow that never lifts.

### The Vimana Spectator Subculture

A sprawling subculture exists around the consumption and analysis of Vimana cycle data. Despite the grim reality that the cycles represent, many people engage with them as spectators — studying past matches, analyzing fighter performance, debating strategy, and maintaining elaborate ranking systems.

This is not entirely callous. For many, spectatorship is a form of preparation — the more that is understood about the Vimana's arenas, challenges, and patterns, the better prepared future fighters will be. The Witness Corps publishes cycle data openly, and the analytical community that has grown around this data represents some of the most dedicated tactical thinkers on the planet.

But there is also an element of escapism. The Vimana is the most dramatic event in human existence. Watching it — even through the limited data that the Pantheon makes available — is compelling in a way that transcends the horror. The spectator community processes this tension in different ways. Some are reverent. Some are clinical. Some are openly addicted to the drama. All of them are, in their own way, trying to make sense of something that resists sense-making.

## The Technology Landscape

Humanity's current technology base is a patchwork of Pantheon-rewarded systems, reverse-engineered derivatives, and pre-cycle remnants. The distribution is wildly uneven, with arcology populations possessing technology that would seem magical to rural communities surviving on pre-industrial tools.

### Energy Weapons

The most visible Pantheon-derived technology. Energy weapons operate by manipulating focused electromagnetic discharge through torsion-cast focusing arrays. They are lethal, reliable, and require ammunition only in the sense that their power cells eventually deplete. Arcology militias carry them as standard equipment. Guild fighters train with them extensively. Rural communities occasionally acquire them through trade, though the power cell supply chain makes sustained use impractical outside arcology infrastructure.

### Medical Technology

Advanced healing and regenerative treatments represent one of the most impactful cycle rewards. Arcology medical facilities can regenerate damaged tissue, repair neurological trauma, and treat conditions that would have been fatal in any previous era. This technology explains the seemingly impossible resilience of trained fighters — their bodies are supported by a medical infrastructure that can undo damage that would kill an unaugmented human in minutes.

Outside arcologies, medical care ranges from competent to primitive. Coastal communities typically have trained medical practitioners with limited pharmaceutical supplies. Underground cities have developed effective herbal and mineral-based treatments through centuries of isolation. Equatorial villages make do with what they have. The disparity in medical access is one of the sharpest indicators of the technology divide.

### Materials Science

The torsion-cast alloys that form the basis of arcology construction are lightweight, extraordinarily strong, and possess inherent radiation-shielding properties. They cannot be manufactured without access to Pantheon-rewarded processing equipment, which means they are effectively controlled by arcology populations. Smaller community structures are built from conventional materials — concrete, steel, reclaimed pre-cycle components — and are correspondingly more vulnerable to environmental hazards.

### Bio-Engineering

Genetic modification, artificial human creation, and cybernetic symbiosis represent the frontier of human technological capability. Bio-engineering is practiced primarily within arcology facilities and augmentation guild research centers. It is the most ethically fraught area of current technology — the creation of life designed for combat raises fundamental questions about consent, personhood, and what it means to be human in a universe that treats humanity as a resource.

### The One Unearned Reward: Faster-Than-Light Travel

Every cycle reward catalog is documented by the Witness Corps. Every technology, every material, every medical advance — all recorded, all studied, all reverse-engineered to the extent possible. But one reward has appeared in the records but has never, not once in over seven thousand cycles, been granted to humanity.

**Faster-than-light travel.**

It is the most coveted prize. It represents escape — the ability to leave this world, reach new stars, establish a human presence beyond the Vimana's reach. Every cycle, some fighters are told that FTL is a possible reward. Every cycle, it is withheld. The Scholar Caste debates whether the Pantheon possesses FTL technology at all, whether it is reserved for a milestone that has not yet been reached, or whether it is offered as motivation — a mirage designed to ensure continued participation.

The long game, as the Scholars call it, is the belief that sufficient accumulated rewards and cycle victories will eventually unlock FTL. It is a belief that requires faith in the Pantheon's good faith — a faith that many find impossible to sustain. But the alternative is accepting that humanity will remain on this damaged planet, under this alien structure, until the cycles end through some other mechanism entirely.

Until then, the cycles continue. The Invitation comes. Fighters are selected. They enter the Vimana. Some return. Most do not. And humanity endures — fractured, uneven, battered, and stubbornly, persistently alive — on a planet that no longer resembles the one it was born on, under the shadow of a structure it cannot escape.
