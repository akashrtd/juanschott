---

This document presents the chronological history of the Vimana cycles and their impact on Earth. This is the authoritative timeline that all lore must be consistent with. No supplementary document, character biography, environmental narrative, or seasonal content may contradict the events, sequences, or outcomes described below. Where ambiguity exists, it is intentional and flagged for future development.

All dates prior to Cycle 0 use the designation **BVC** (Before Vimana Contact). Dates after Cycle 0 are denoted by cycle number. Linear year equivalents are unavailable — the orbital shifts caused by Vimana interaction rendered pre-contact calendars unreliable, and the Arcology Council never standardized a replacement.

---

## Era 1: Before the Vimana (Pre-Cycle)

**Span:** Unknown duration — estimated several millennia of recorded human civilization prior to First Contact.

Earth's technological ceiling had reached what historians now call the **Late Asymptote** — a period of extreme material capability coupled with deep political stagnation. Fusion power, orbital manufacturing, quantum computing, and weather engineering were all mature technologies. Humanity had colonized its own solar system in a limited capacity: research stations on Mars and Europa, automated mining across the asteroid belt, and a permanent lunar habitation complex housing roughly forty thousand people.

None of it was coordinated.

The planet was governed by a patchwork of supranational blocs, corporate territories, and sovereign holdouts. No single authority controlled more than a third of Earth's surface infrastructure. Treaties existed; trust did not. Military technology had plateaued at conventional munitions, drone warfare, and orbital kinetic weapons. There were no energy-based weapons. No bio-enhancement beyond therapeutic gene therapy. No contact with any intelligence beyond Earth.

The species was alone, fractious, and powerful — a combination that bred complacency.

The historical record from this era is fragmentary. The orbital shifts and ecological damage of later cycles destroyed vast archives. What survives comes primarily from hardened data vaults beneath the original Arcology foundations and oral traditions preserved by the earliest fighter guilds.

What is known with certainty is this: humanity did not see the Vimana coming. No telescope detected it. No sensor array registered its approach. One moment the sky was empty. The next, it was not.

## Era 2: First Contact (Cycle 0)

**Span:** Approximately 11 months from Vimana arrival to Vimana departure.

### The Appearance

The Vimana materialized in high Earth orbit without warning. It did not enter the system — it simply *was*. Sensor logs from surviving orbital installations describe an object of impossible geometry, shifting between configurations that human instrumentation could not resolve. Visual observation from the ground was clearer: a vast, luminous structure, visible to the naked eye even in daylight, hanging above the planet like a second moon made of folded light.

Panic was immediate and global. Markets collapsed within hours. Three regional conflicts halted mid-engagement as military forces redeployed aimlessly. Religious movements erupted across every continent. The supranational blocs attempted a coordinated scientific response but were hampered by mutual suspicion and the sheer impossibility of the phenomenon.

### The Invitation

Sixty-three days after the Vimana's appearance, every human being on Earth — and aboard every off-world installation — received **The Invitation** simultaneously. It was not transmitted through any known medium. It bypassed screens, speakers, and neural interfaces entirely. It arrived inside consciousness itself.

Initial reception was chaotic. Each individual experienced The Invitation differently: some heard a voice, some saw text, some perceived abstract geometric patterns that resolved into meaning upon reflection. But the *content* was identical across all eight billion recipients.

The Invitation communicated the following, rendered here in the closest available paraphrase:

> *You have been selected. A contest will be held. You will send fighters into the Vimana. If you win, you will receive a reward. If you lose, a penalty will be exacted. The contest will repeat until you earn the final prize or until you can no longer compete.*

The message included rules of engagement — where fighters would be sent, how combat would be structured, what constituted victory and defeat. It was precise where precision served and maddeningly vague everywhere else. Most critically, it did not explain *why*.

Within hours of The Invitation, the Vimana opened its first **entry point** — a visible aperture in its structure that sensor probes confirmed was a stable ingress route.

### The First Deployment

There was no time to prepare. The Invitation had included a deadline: Earth had 120 days to send fighters into the Vimana, or the penalty would be applied automatically.

The blocs assembled a coalition force from their best military personnel. Special operators, combat pilots, elite soldiers — the most dangerous humans alive. They were equipped with the finest conventional weaponry Earth could produce: kinetic firearms, explosive ordnance, tactical drones, powered exoskeletons. They were given no intelligence on what they would face inside.

Of the 2,400 fighters who entered the Vimana on that first deployment, 187 returned.

They brought back fragmentary, traumatized accounts of environments that defied physics — shifting architecture, combatants of unknown biology, weapons that operated on principles no human science could replicate. The coalition fighters had been outmatched at every level. Their weapons had been ineffective. Their tactics had been irrelevant. They had been, by every meaningful measure, children fighting gods.

### The Penalty

The Vimana declared Earth's defeat and enacted the penalty: **orbital displacement**. Earth's orbit was shifted. Not by much — the surviving astronomical data suggests a reduction of approximately 0.3% in orbital radius — but enough to be measured, enough to alter global climate patterns, enough to signal that the Vimana's capabilities were not symbolic.

The Vimana departed. It did not accelerate away. It simply ceased to be present.

Earth was left to reckon with the reality of its situation: the Vimana would return. There would be another contest. And humanity was not ready.

## Era 3: Early Cycles (Cycles 1–5)

**Span:** Approximately 30 years (estimated — calendar disruption makes precision impossible).

The period between Vimana departures and returns became known as the **Interim**. Humanity learned quickly that the Vimana operated on a roughly consistent schedule, returning approximately every five to seven years. This gave Earth's civilizations time to prepare — barely.

### Cycle 1

- **Outcome:** Loss
- **Reward/Punishment:** Orbital displacement (cumulative). Earth's orbit tightened by an additional estimated 0.4%.
- **Impact:** Climate destabilization accelerated. Equatorial regions experienced sustained heat events. Agricultural output dropped 12% globally. The first climate refugee crises began.
- **Notable Events:** The **First Guild Compact** was signed between surviving Cycle 0 fighters and the coalition governments. The Compact established the guild system: independent training organizations that would specialize in Vimana combat, funded by international mandate but operationally autonomous. The underlying logic was that conventional military structures had failed and a new paradigm was needed.
- **Notable Fighters:** [TBD — Season 1 environmental storytelling hook]

Humanity sent 3,100 fighters. Approximately 400 returned. Slightly better than Cycle 0 — enough to confirm that experience and preparation mattered, but not nearly enough to win.

### Cycle 2

- **Outcome:** Victory
- **Reward:** **Advanced Materials Science** — Knowledge of metastable exotic matter compounds, programmable metamaterials, and structural engineering principles far beyond human capability.
- **Impact:** This was the turning point. The reward was not a weapon — it was a foundation. Programmable metamaterials allowed the construction of structures that were lightweight, self-repairing, and nearly indestructible by conventional means. Within two Interims, the first **Arcology** was completed in [TBD — see earth_and_humanity.md for Arcology locations]. Arcologies were enclosed, climate-independent megastructures capable of housing millions. They were humanity's insurance policy against a dying planet.
- **Notable Events:** The victory validated the guild system. Enrollment surged. Fighter training became the most prestigious profession on Earth.
- **Notable Fighters:** [TBD — foundational guild hero, potential Season 1 character ancestor]

### Cycle 3

- **Outcome:** Loss
- **Reward/Punishment:** Orbital displacement (cumulative, accelerating). Additional estimated 0.6% orbital reduction. Total orbital shift from baseline now approximately 1.3%.
- **Impact:** Significant ecological damage. Ocean current patterns disrupted. The first Arcology — still underpopulated — became a refuge for climate evacuees. Political tensions between Arcology residents and "externals" (those still living outside) began to crystallize into lasting social stratification.
- **Notable Events:** Post-defeat analysis revealed that Vimana combat environments had become more complex. The Vimana was escalating its challenges in response to humanity's improvements. This was the first confirmation that the contest was *adaptive*.
- **Notable Fighters:** [TBD]

### Cycle 4

- **Outcome:** Victory
- **Reward:** **Bio-Enhancement** — Comprehensive understanding of genetic modification, accelerated tissue engineering, and biological optimization.
- **Impact:** The reward opened the door to directed human evolution. Therapeutic applications were immediate: disease resistance, extended longevity, enhanced physical performance. But the real revolution was in fighter augmentation. Guilds began developing optimized combat biologies — stronger, faster, more resilient humans designed specifically for Vimana deployment.
- **Notable Events:** The **First Artificial Human** was created in a guild research facility. Designated [TBD], this being was grown from engineered genetic stock rather than born. It was fully sentient, legally human under Guild Compact provisions, and possessed physical capabilities exceeding baseline humanity by a significant margin. The ethical debate was immediate and fierce. It never fully resolved.
- **Notable Fighters:** [TBD — first artificial human fighter]

### Cycle 5

- **Outcome:** Victory
- **Reward:** **Energy Manipulation** — Principles of directed energy generation, containment, and weaponization.
- **Impact:** Energy weapons became possible for the first time. Coil-guns, plasma projectors, particle lances — an entirely new category of armament replaced conventional kinetics as the fighter's primary tools. The gap between guild-trained fighters and conventional military forces widened into a chasm.
- **Notable Events:** The first **energy weapons** were deployed in Cycle 5's closing engagements. After-action reports confirmed a dramatic increase in fighter effectiveness. For the first time, humanity felt it was learning the Vimana's language — not just surviving it, but speaking back.

## Era 4: The Transformation (Cycles 6–15)

**Span:** Approximately 70 years.

The rewards accumulated. Humanity did not simply receive gifts — it received *tools*, each one building on the last, each one changing the species that wielded it.

### Genetic Divergence

Bio-Enhancement (Cycle 4) and subsequent rewards led to the emergence of **humanoid subspecies** — populations whose genetic modifications had become heritable and distinct. By Cycle 10, the fighter population included:

- **Baseline Humans** — Unmodified or therapeutically enhanced. Still the majority of Earth's total population.
- **Artificial Humans** — Lab-grown, genetically optimized. Legally recognized as human. Culturally contested.
- **Adapted Humanoids** — Naturally born humans whose lineage included multiple generations of bio-enhancement. Physiologically distinct: enhanced density, altered metabolism, improved reflex architecture.
- **Cybersymbiotes** — Beings who had integrated Pantheon-derived cybernetic systems into their biology. The boundary between organic and synthetic was, for them, meaningless. Emerged primarily after Cycle 8, when study of recovered Pantheon combat remnants yielded functional cybernetic principles.

The guild system adapted. Guilds that had once been training camps became *lineages* — traditions of combat philosophy, biological optimization, and technological specialization passed from one generation of fighters to the next. Some guilds accepted only baselines. Others specialized in artificial humans. A few embraced radical diversity.

### Arcology Expansion

Advanced Materials Science (Cycle 2) and subsequent material rewards allowed Arcology construction to accelerate dramatically. By Cycle 15, approximately 40% of Earth's surviving population lived within Arcology complexes. The remaining 60% existed in the **External Zones** — the shrinking habitable corridors between Arcologies, where climate damage was managed but never fully reversed.

Arcologies became city-states. They developed their own governance structures, their own economies, their own cultures. The international blocs that had dominated pre-Vimana politics faded into irrelevance. Power flowed to whoever controlled Arcology infrastructure and guild access.

### Cultural Schisms

The Transformation Era was defined by three persistent cultural fractures:

1. **The Bio-Ethics Divide** — Whether bio-enhancement and artificial human creation were legitimate survival strategies or violations of human dignity. This debate never reached consensus. It hardened into ideology.
2. **The Arcology Divide** — Whether Arcology residents and External populations shared a common future or were diverging into separate civilizations. Resource allocation disputes were constant.
3. **The Purpose Debate** — Whether humanity's goal should be to survive the Vimana cycles indefinitely or to win the final prize — **FTL travel** — and leave Earth behind entirely.

- **Impact:** Fighter training became exponentially more efficient. A guild fighter could now master in months what previously took years. Spillover into civilian applications was limited but significant — medical diagnostics, engineering optimization, scientific research all benefited.

#### Cycle 8
- **Outcome:** Victory
- **Reward:** **Cybernetic Symbiosis Principles** — Knowledge of how to integrate synthetic systems with organic biology at the cellular level.
- **Impact:** The first cybersymbiotes were created. Unlike prosthetics or implants, cybernetic symbiosis produced beings whose organic and synthetic systems were interdependent and co-evolved. This was the most controversial reward to date, exceeding even bio-enhancement in its capacity to disturb traditionalists.

More importantly, it suggested that the Vimana's reward system was *escalating toward something* — each reward more powerful, more fundamental, more transformative than the last.

## Era 5: The Present Day (Current Cycle — Cycle [TBD])

**Cycle Number:** [TBD — to be determined for launch]

Earth is damaged but functional. The planet's orbit has been shifted by approximately 5% from its pre-Vimana baseline — enough to cause significant ecological damage, but not enough to render the world uninhabitable. Arcology complexes house the majority of the population. The External Zones persist in shrinking temperate corridors, sustained by environmental engineering that requires constant maintenance.

Humanity has won roughly **60% of all completed cycles**. This figure is deliberately imprecise — different sources count victories differently, and several cycles had contested or ambiguous outcomes. The exact total is [TBD for future storytelling consistency].

### The Current State

The fighter population is the most diverse in human history. Baseline humans fight alongside artificial humans, adapted humanoids, and cybersymbiotes. Guild lineages span centuries of accumulated combat philosophy. Energy weapons, gravitational engineering, and bio-enhancement have produced fighters whose capabilities would have seemed godlike to pre-Vimana humanity.

And yet, the Vimana still wins roughly four times out of every ten engagements. The contest has not gotten easier. The Vimana adapts. It escalates. Every victory yields a reward that changes humanity — and every change introduces new vulnerabilities, new dependencies, new questions about what the species is becoming.

### FTL: The Final Prize

The Invitation mentioned a "final prize." Guild scholars and Arcology research councils have concluded, based on the escalating pattern of rewards, that the ultimate reward is **faster-than-light travel** — the ability to leave Earth, leave the solar system, and potentially leave the Vimana's reach entirely.

FTL remains unearned. The number of consecutive victories required to trigger the final reward is unknown. Estimates range from three to seven consecutive cycle wins. No civilization — as far as humanity knows — has ever achieved it.

The debate over FTL divides humanity into two camps:

- **The Departure Faction** believes humanity should pursue FTL aggressively, accept the risks of pushing for consecutive victories, and leave Earth and the Vimana behind.
- **The Stewardship Faction** believes the priority should be sustainable survival — winning enough cycles to maintain Earth's habitability without gambling on FTL.

This debate defines the political landscape of the current cycle.

### The Vimana Has Returned

The current cycle has begun. The Vimana materialized [TBD — relative to game launch narrative]. Entry points have opened. Fighters are deploying.

**This is where the game takes place.**

## Unresolved Mysteries

The following questions have no canonical answers. They are flagged for future development and must not be prematurely resolved in any lore content without explicit narrative team approval.

### Why does the Pantheon do this?

The Pantheon — the intelligence or intelligences behind the Vimana — has never communicated its motivations. The Invitation was transactional: fight, win, receive. No explanation of purpose, no statement of philosophy, no indication of whether the contest is a test, a punishment, a harvest, an entertainment, or something entirely beyond human conceptual frameworks.

Competing theories exist in-universe:

- **The Crucible Theory:** The Pantheon is accelerating species development through structured adversity. The rewards are not gifts — they are a curriculum.
- **The Harvest Theory:** The Pantheon is farming technological innovation. Each cycle produces new knowledge that the Pantheon extracts.
- **The Predator Theory:** The Vimana is a weapon system. The "contest" is a targeting process. Species that become strong enough to win FTL become something else — something the Pantheon wants.
- **The Incomprehensible Theory:** The Pantheon's motivations are genuinely beyond human cognition. Searching for anthropic explanations is a category error.

**Resolution Status:** Planned for [TBD — future season]. Not to be addressed before Season [TBD].

### Has any civilization ever refused to fight?

The Invitation includes a provision for refusal: a civilization may choose not to send fighters. The penalty for refusal is unstated but implied to be catastrophic. No human authority has tested this.

**Resolution Status:** Planned for [TBD]. This question may be central to a future season's narrative arc.

### What happens if a civilization wins FTL and leaves — does the Vimana follow?

Unknown. The Vimana has never explicitly stated that FTL represents *escape*. It may represent *graduation* into a different phase of engagement. Or it may represent genuine liberation. The uncertainty is narratively productive and should be preserved until [TBD].

### Is there a way to communicate with the Pantheon beyond fighting?

No confirmed method exists. Some fighters have reported anomalous experiences during Vimana deployment — perceptions of being observed, of encountering non-hostile intelligences within the Vimana's structure, of receiving communications that did not conform to The Invitation's format. These reports are unverified and inconsistent. They may be combat stress, genuine contact, or deliberate manipulation by the Vimana itself.

**Resolution Status:** Environmental storytelling hook for [TBD — specific map/season]. Fighters' anomalous reports should be seeded as background lore in future content.

### Are there other civilizations currently fighting in their own Vimanas?

Unknown. The Vimana has never referenced other participants. No alien intelligence has been encountered within the Vimana's combat environments — all opponents have been non-sentient constructs or automated systems. However, the scale of the Vimana's structure suggests it is not dedicated to a single species.

**Resolution Status:** Planned for [TBD — late-season narrative expansion].
